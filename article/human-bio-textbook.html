Human BIO Textbook
Thursday, September 4, 2025
12:44 PM

Overview:

The document serves as a comprehensive textbook on human biology, emphasizing the relevance of biological concepts to everyday life and societal challenges. It integrates digital learning resources and addresses contemporary topics such as climate change, biodiversity loss, and emerging diseases.

Key Content Areas and Features:

 

  • About the Authors and Contributors:
    • Sylvia S. Mader: Nationally recognized biology text author, educated at Bryn Mawr, Harvard, Tufts, and Nova Southeastern. Known for her crisp and entertaining writing style.
    • Michael Windelspecht: Educator with experience teaching introductory biology, genetics, and human genetics. Holds degrees from Michigan State and the University of South Florida. Focuses on data analysis from digital learning platforms and developing personalized microlearning assets. Has authored and edited over 30 reference textbooks. Manages the Ricochet Science website.
    • Dave Cox: Professor of biology at Lincoln Land Community College. Teaches introductory biology, marine biology, and biological field studies. Contributor to multiple McGraw Hill titles and develops educational resources for ecotourism.

 

  • Digital Learning and Instructor/Student Resources:
    • McGraw Hill Connect: A platform offering tools for instructors (course building, auto-grading, LMS integration, reports) and students (adaptive study resources like SmartBook 2.0, ReadAnywhere app for offline access, digital eBook, assignments).
    • ISTUDY/SUPPORT AT every step: Emphasizes flexible and intuitive tools to maximize study time and meet individual learning needs.
    • Virtual Labs and Lab Simulations: Designed to help students learn practical and conceptual skills for online or in-person lab experiences, with adaptive pre-lab and post-lab assessments.
    • Relevancy Modules: Supplementary eBooks accompanying each unit, connecting biological content to societal topics. Each module includes an introductory video, scientific concepts overview, application, infographic, and discussion/assessment questions. Examples include "The Biology of Chocolate," "COVID-19: The Rise of a Global Pandemic," and "Climate Change."
    • BioNOW Videos: Narrated and produced by Jason Carlson, these videos offer a relevant, applied approach to help students relate biology to their daily lives.
    • Ricochet Science Website: Managed by Michael Windelspecht, this site (ricochetscience.com) provides updates on science news, PopScience articles for classroom discussions, videos, and tutorial animations.

 

  • Goals of the Seventeenth Edition (Preface):
    • Make content more relevant by updating chapter openers and themed readings to focus on current issues.
    • Integrate relevancy modules.
    • Redesign artwork for digital mobile devices.
    • Develop a new Featured Reading Series: "The Diversity of Science," highlighting unrecognized scientists.

 

  • Brief and Full Contents:
    The textbook is organized into 7 units and 25 chapters, covering fundamental aspects of human biology.
    • Unit 1: Human Organization (Ch 1-4): Exploring Life and Science, Chemistry of Life, Cell Structure and Function, Tissues, Organ Systems, and Homeostasis.
    • Unit 2: Maintenance of the Human Body (Ch 5-11): Cardiovascular System (Heart and Blood Vessels, Blood), Lymphatic and Immune Systems, Biology of Infectious Diseases, Digestive System and Nutrition, Respiratory System, Urinary System.
    • Unit 3: Movement and Support in Humans (Ch 12-13): Skeletal System, Muscular System.
    • Unit 4: Integration and Coordination in Humans (Ch 14-16): Nervous System, Senses, Endocrine System.
    • Unit 5: Reproduction in Humans (Ch 17-18): Reproductive System, Development and Aging.
    • Unit 6: Human Genetics (Ch 19-22): Cell Division, Cancer, Genetic Inheritance, DNA Biology and Technology.
    • Unit 7: Human Evolution and Ecology (Ch 23-25): Human Evolution, Ecology and the Nature of Ecosystems, Human Interactions with the Biosphere.

 

  • Readings (Special Features): The book includes "BIOLOGY TODAY" sections categorized into Diversity in Science, Health, and Science. These provide real-world examples and deeper dives into specific topics. Examples include:
    • Diversity in Science: The Legacy of Marie Curie, Nettie Stevens and the Discovery of the Sex Chromosomes.
    • Health: Fiber in the Diet, Good and Bad Cholesterol, Recent Findings in Preventing Heart Disease, COVID-19.
    • Science: Adapting to Life at High Elevations, Discovering the Cause of Ulcers, Artificial Lungs, Genetics of Eye Color, The Complexity of Our Evolutionary Past.

 

  • Core Concepts and Themes (from Chapter 1 summary):
    • Characteristics of Life: Organization (atoms to biosphere), acquisition of materials and energy (metabolism, photosynthesis), reproduction and development (DNA, genes, mutations), homeostasis, response to stimuli, and evolutionary history (natural selection, adaptation).
    • Humans and Other Animals: Humans are Eukarya, Kingdom Animalia (primate mammals), sharing a common apelike ancestor with apes. Emphasis on human cultural heritage (language, tool use, values).
    • Science as a Process (Scientific Method): Observation, hypothesis development (inductive reasoning), hypothesis testing (experimentation, deductive reasoning, experimental/control groups, model organisms/systems), data collection and analysis (graphs, statistical significance), conclusion, and scientific publications (peer review). The concept of scientific theory (e.g., Cell Theory, Gene Theory, Theory of Evolution) as accepted explanations.
    • Science and Societal Challenges: Distinction between science and technology. Key challenges include climate change (global warming, CO2 emissions), biodiversity and habitat loss (extinction rates, threat to rainforests and coral reefs), and emerging/reemerging diseases (avian influenza, SARS, MERS, COVID-19, Ebola).
